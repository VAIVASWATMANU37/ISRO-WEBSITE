Mazda BP-ZE

The Mazda BP-ZE is a 1.8-liter DOHC inline-four engine that became a cult favorite thanks to its rev-happy nature, solid construction, and tuning potential. Introduced in the early 1990s, it featured a cast iron block, aluminum 16-valve head, and a forged crankshaft, making it both durable and responsive. With a square bore and stroke (83mm x 85mm), it produced between 128 to 146 horsepower in naturally aspirated form, and was famously used in the Mazda MX-5 Miata (NA8) and 323 Familia. The BP-ZE also included performance-minded features like piston oil squirters, a structural aluminum oil pan, and Mazda’s Variable Inertia Charging System (VICS) for improved high-RPM breathing. It’s a beloved engine among enthusiasts for its balance of reliability, simplicity, and spirited character.
