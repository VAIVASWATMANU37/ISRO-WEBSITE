BMW M50/M52

The BMW M50 and M52 engines are revered inline-six units that symbolize BMW’s engineering prowess in the 1990s. The M50 debuted with a cast iron block and dual overhead cams, offering up to 192 hp and seamless power delivery, especially after the introduction of VANOS in 1992. It laid the groundwork for the more refined M52, which featured an aluminum block, improved fuel efficiency, and torque—peaking at 193 hp and 280 Nm in its 2.8L variant. While both are prized for their smooth operation and tuning potential, the M50 is favored for simplicity and robust build, and the M52 for its everyday drivability and technical evolution. Together, they remain favorites among enthusiasts for engine swaps, turbo builds, and classic BMW restorations.
