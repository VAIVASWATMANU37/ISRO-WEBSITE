Honda B18C/B16B

The Honda B18C and B16B are two legendary engines from Honda’s B-series lineup, revered by enthusiasts for their high-revving nature and performance pedigree. The B16B, found in the Civic Type R (EK9), is a 1.6L DOHC VTEC engine that produces an impressive 185 hp at 8,200 rpm, thanks to its high 10.8:1 compression ratio and aggressive cam profiles. It’s essentially a destroked version of the B18C Type R engine, using a taller block and longer rods to achieve a high rod-to-stroke ratio, allowing it to scream to a redline of 8,400 rpm. On the other hand, the B18C, particularly in its Type R form, is a 1.8L powerhouse delivering up to 197 hp and 131 lb-ft of torque. It’s known for its broader torque band and slightly more usable power in daily driving or track conditions. While the B16B is a marvel of engineering with its high-revving thrills, the B18C offers more grunt and is often favored for its tuning potential and versatility. Both engines are icons, representing Honda’s golden era of naturally aspirated performance.
