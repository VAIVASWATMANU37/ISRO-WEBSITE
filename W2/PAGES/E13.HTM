Alfa Romeo Busso V6

The **Alfa Romeo Busso V6** is a 60-degree V6 engine that stands as a symbol of Italian automotive passion and engineering artistry. Designed by Giuseppe Busso and produced from 1979 to 2005, it featured an **aluminum block and head**, with configurations ranging from **2.0 to 3.2 liters** and both **SOHC and DOHC** valvetrains over its lifespan. Revered for its **distinctive intake growl** and **silky power delivery**, the Busso V6 powered iconic models like the **Alfa 75, GTV6, 164, and 156 GTA**. Its mechanical beauty and emotional resonance earned it nicknames like the “**Violin of Arese**,” and it’s often hailed as one of the best-sounding V6 engines ever made. Even years after its retirement, the Busso V6 remains a beloved masterpiece among enthusiasts and collectors.
