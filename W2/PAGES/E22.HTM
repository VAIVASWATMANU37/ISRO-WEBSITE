Cosworth YB

The Cosworth YB engine is a legendary turbocharged inline-four powerhouse developed by Cosworth for Ford in the mid-1980s. Based on the Ford Pinto block, it debuted in the 1986 Ford Sierra RS Cosworth and quickly became a motorsport icon. With a 16-valve aluminum head, robust internals, and a Garrett turbocharger, the YB was capable of producing over 200 bhp in road trim and well beyond 500 bhp in race-spec RS500 form. Its adaptability and tuning potential made it a favorite among rally and touring car teams, dominating Group A racing through the late '80s and early '90s. Even today, the YB remains a cult favorite among performance enthusiasts and classic Ford tuners.
