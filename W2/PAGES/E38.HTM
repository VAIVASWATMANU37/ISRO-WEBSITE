BMW S54

The BMW S54 is a high-revving 3.2-liter naturally aspirated inline-six engine that powered some of BMW’s most iconic M models between 2000 and 2006, including the E46 M3, Z3 M, and Z4 M. It delivers up to 333 horsepower and 262 lb-ft of torque, with a screaming 8,000 rpm redline, making it one of the most celebrated engines in BMW’s history. Engineered with individual throttle bodies, double VANOS variable valve timing, and a forged steel crankshaft, the S54 blends race-inspired tech with everyday drivability. Its cast iron block and aluminum head ensure durability, while the MSS54 ECU manages precise ignition and fuel delivery. Though revered for its performance, the S54 demands meticulous maintenance. Common issues include VANOS failures, rod bearing wear, and cooling system weaknesses, especially in early production models. Still, with proper care, it’s known to exceed 200,000 miles while retaining its thrilling character. The S54 is often hailed as the last great naturally aspirated BMW inline-six, and its legacy lives on in enthusiast circles, engine swaps, and track builds.
