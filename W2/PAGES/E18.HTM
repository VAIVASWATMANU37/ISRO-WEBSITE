MW S65

The BMW S65 is a 4.0-liter naturally aspirated V8 engine that powered the E90/E92/E93 M3 from 2007 to 2013, and it remains one of BMW’s most celebrated powerplants. Derived from the S85 V10 used in the M5, the S65 shares its high-revving character, individual throttle bodies, and aluminum construction, but with a more compact V8 layout. It produces 414 horsepower at 8,300 rpm and 295 lb-ft of torque at 3,900 rpm, with a screaming 8,400 rpm redline that delivers a visceral driving experience. The engine features double VANOS, a quasi-dry sump oiling system, and a lightweight design that actually made it lighter than the inline-six it replaced. Though it’s revered for its performance and sound, the S65 is also known for rod bearing wear and other maintenance quirks, making proper upkeep essential. Still, for many enthusiasts, the S65 represents the pinnacle of naturally aspirated M power.
