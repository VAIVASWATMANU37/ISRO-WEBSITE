Mercedes M104

The Mercedes-Benz M104 is a naturally aspirated inline-six petrol engine produced from 1988 to 1999, celebrated for its blend of refinement, durability, and performance. Available in 2.8L, 3.0L, and 3.2L variants—with rare AMG-tuned 3.4L and 3.6L versions—it featured a cast iron block, aluminum DOHC head, and four valves per cylinder with variable valve timing on the intake side. Power output ranged from 190 to 276 hp, with torque peaking at 284 lb-ft, depending on displacement and tuning. The M104 powered a wide array of Mercedes models, including the W124 E-Class, W140 S-Class, R129 SL, and W210 E-Class, as well as SsangYong vehicles like the Chairman and Rexton. Known for its silky-smooth operation and long lifespan—often exceeding 300,000 km with proper care—the M104 is still revered by enthusiasts. However, it’s not without quirks: oil leaks, cooling system failures, and mass airflow sensor issues are common in aging units. Despite these, the M104 remains a benchmark of Mercedes engineering, offering a compelling mix of classic straight-six charm and modern reliability.
