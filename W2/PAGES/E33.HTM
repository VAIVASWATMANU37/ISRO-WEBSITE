Suzuki G13B

The Suzuki G13B is a legendary 1.3-liter DOHC inline-four engine that earned its stripes in the late '80s and early '90s, especially in performance-oriented models like the Suzuki Swift GTi and Cultus GTi. With a displacement of 1,298 cc, it featured 16 valves, multi-point fuel injection, and a relatively high compression ratio of 10.0–11.5:1, allowing it to produce between 100–115 horsepower and 109–112 Nm of torque. Built with an aluminum block and head, the G13B was lightweight and rev-happy, with a redline approaching 7,600 rpm. It lacked hydraulic lifters, so valve clearance had to be manually adjusted every 30,000 km, and it used a timing belt rather than a chain. While it didn’t have a turbocharger or phase regulator, its DOHC setup and free-flowing head design made it a favorite among tuners and motorsport enthusiasts—especially in India, where it powered rally-spec Esteems and swapped Zens. Despite its age, the G13B is still revered for its simplicity, reliability, and tuning potential. With bolt-on mods, it can comfortably push beyond 130 hp, and its compact size makes it ideal for engine swaps in lightweight platforms. Just don’t let it overheat—cracks in the cooling jacket or cylinder head can be costly.
