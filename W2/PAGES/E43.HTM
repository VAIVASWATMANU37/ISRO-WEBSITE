Ford Cologne 2.9 I6

The **Ford Cologne 2.9L engine** is actually a **V6**, not an inline-six (I6), and it’s part of Ford’s long-running Cologne engine family produced in Germany. Introduced in the mid-1980s, this 60° V6 was a refinement of the earlier 2.8L version, featuring a **chain-driven camshaft**, improved **three-port exhaust heads**, and **electronic fuel injection** via Bosch’s L-Jetronic system. Used in vehicles like the **Ford Ranger**, **Bronco II**, **Sierra XR4x4**, and **Granada**, the 2.9L V6 delivered around **140–160 horsepower** and **170–230 lb-ft of torque**, depending on the market and model. Its robust cast iron block and aluminum heads made it a durable choice, though early North American versions were prone to **cylinder head cracking** and **valvetrain oiling issues**, which were largely resolved in later models with redesigned heads marked “89TM.” Despite its quirks, the Cologne 2.9L V6 earned a loyal following thanks to its **modifiability**, with aftermarket turbo kits and performance upgrades available from companies like Janspeed and Turbo Technics. It remains a nostalgic favorite among enthusiasts of classic Ford trucks and European performance sedans.
