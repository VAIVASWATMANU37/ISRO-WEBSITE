Volvo B6304

The Volvo B6304 is a 3.0-liter inline-six petrol engine that played a pivotal role in Volvo’s transition to more refined powertrains during the 1990s. Part of the brand’s modular engine family, it featured a 24-valve DOHC configuration and was known for its smooth performance and durability. Initially introduced in models like the Volvo 960, S90, and V90, the B6304 came in several variants, including naturally aspirated (B6304S) and turbocharged versions (B6304T2, T4, T5). These engines delivered between 204 to 350 horsepower depending on the configuration, with the turbocharged T5 variant offering up to 500 Nm of torque. Built with aluminum block and head, and equipped with fuel injection, the B6304 was praised for its balance of power and efficiency, making it a favorite among enthusiasts and a solid performer in Volvo’s lineup.
