Toyota 4A-GE 20V

The Toyota 4A-GE 20V is a high-revving, naturally aspirated 1.6-liter inline-four engine that represents the pinnacle of Toyota’s A-series performance lineage. Introduced in the early 1990s, it came in two main variants: the Silver Top (AE101) and the more refined Black Top (AE111). Both featured an advanced five-valves-per-cylinder layout (three intake, two exhaust), individual throttle bodies (ITBs), and Variable Valve Timing (VVT) on the intake cam—cutting-edge tech for its time. Producing up to 165 hp at 7800 rpm, the 4A-GE 20V delivered razor-sharp throttle response and a thrilling top-end rush, making it a favorite in lightweight platforms like the Corolla Levin, Sprinter Trueno, and Toyota MR2. Its cast-iron block and Yamaha-designed aluminum head offered a blend of durability and precision, while the ITBs gave it a distinctive intake roar that enthusiasts still chase today. Revered for its balance, reliability, and rev-happy nature, the 4A-GE 20V remains a cult classic in the world of naturally aspirated performance engines.
