Renault F4R NA

The Renault F4R NA is a naturally aspirated 2.0-liter inline-four petrol engine that’s part of Renault’s long-running F-series. Introduced in the early 1990s and produced well into the 2020s, it’s known for its durability, simplicity, and broad application across models like the Renault Megane, Laguna, Espace, Duster, and even the Nissan Terrano. This DOHC 16-valve engine features a cast iron block with an aluminum head, fuel injection, and a timing belt-driven valvetrain. Power output typically ranges from 135 to 143 horsepower, with torque figures around 140–195 Nm, depending on the variant. While it lacks turbocharging, some versions include a phase regulator on the intake camshaft to improve efficiency and responsiveness. Despite its reputation for reliability, the F4R isn’t without quirks. Common issues include ignition coil failures, oil consumption, and phase regulator wear—often noticeable by a diesel-like engine sound around 60,000–100,000 km. Still, with proper maintenance and quality fuel, this engine can easily surpass 250,000 km, making it a favorite among DIYers and tuners alike.
