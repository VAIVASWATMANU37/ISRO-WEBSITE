Mazda 13B Rotary

The Mazda 13B Rotary is a legendary 1.3-liter two-rotor Wankel engine that became synonymous with Mazda’s performance identity. Unlike conventional piston engines, it uses rotors spinning in an epitrochoidal housing, allowing for high-revving, compact, and lightweight design. First introduced in the early 1970s, the 13B evolved through multiple iterations—from the naturally aspirated 13B-AP and 13B-RESI, to the turbocharged 13B-T and the iconic 13B-REW, which featured twin sequential turbos in the third-gen RX-7. Known for its smooth power delivery and distinctive exhaust note, the 13B gained a cult following despite its quirks—like apex seal wear and thirsty fuel consumption. Still, its unique character and tuning potential have made it a favorite among enthusiasts, racers, and rotary purists alike.
