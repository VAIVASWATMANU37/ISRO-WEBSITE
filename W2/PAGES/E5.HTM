3S-GTE

The Toyota 3S-GTE is a legendary 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-four engine that earned its stripes in motorsport and street performance alike. Introduced in 1986 and produced until 2007, it was the high-performance variant of Toyota’s S-series engines, featuring a robust cast iron block, aluminum DOHC 16-valve head (designed by Yamaha), and a turbocharger that evolved across five generations. This engine powered icons like the Toyota Celica GT-Four, MR2 Turbo, and Caldina GT-T, delivering between 185 to 260 horsepower depending on the generation. Known for its durability and tuning potential, the 3S-GTE featured forged internals, under-piston oil squirters, and intercoolers (air-to-air or water-to-air depending on the model). Its square bore and stroke (86mm x 86mm) gave it a balanced character, making it a favorite among tuners and rally enthusiasts. In short, the 3S-GTE wasn’t just an engine—it was Toyota’s turbocharged love letter to performance.
