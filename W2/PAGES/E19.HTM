Mercedes M113K

The Mercedes M113K is a 5.4-liter supercharged V8 engine that represents one of AMG’s most iconic powerplants. Based on the naturally aspirated M113, the “K” stands for Kompressor, denoting its roots-type supercharger paired with an air-to-water intercooler, which helped it produce up to 493 horsepower and 516 lb-ft of torque in models like the E55 AMG, SL55 AMG, and S55 AMG. Built with a cast aluminum block, SOHC heads with three valves per cylinder, and forged internals, the M113K was engineered for both brute strength and long-term durability. Its relatively simple design compared to modern twin-turbo setups made it a favorite among tuners for its reliability and massive tuning potential. Even decades later, the M113K remains a benchmark for supercharged V8 performance and a symbol of AMG’s golden era.
