Nissan VQ35DE/HR

The **Nissan VQ35DE and VQ35HR** are 3.5-liter V6 engines from the acclaimed VQ family, known for their smooth power delivery and strong performance pedigree. The **VQ35DE**, introduced in the early 2000s, features an all-aluminum construction with DOHC and variable valve timing, producing up to 298 horsepower in some trims. It powered a wide range of vehicles including the **350Z**, **Altima**, **Maxima**, and several Infiniti models. The **VQ35HR** ("High Rev") succeeded the DE in 2007 with significant upgrades: a higher redline (7,500 rpm), dual intake and exhaust variable valve timing, a reinforced block, and a symmetrical intake manifold. These changes improved breathing, durability, and responsiveness, pushing output to around **306 horsepower**. While both engines are revered, the HR is often favored for its refinement and tuning potential, making it a staple in performance builds and track-day warriors alike.
