Honda J35

The Honda J35 is a 3.5-liter V6 engine from Honda’s J-series family, celebrated for its smooth power delivery, reliability, and versatility across a wide range of vehicles. First introduced in the late 1990s, it features a **60-degree aluminum block**, **SOHC or DOHC heads** (depending on the generation), and Honda’s **VTEC** or **Variable Cylinder Management (VCM)** systems for improved efficiency and performance. Power output ranges from around **210 to over 310 horsepower**, with torque figures up to **275 lb-ft**, making it a strong performer in both sedans and SUVs. Found in models like the **Honda Accord V6**, **Odyssey**, **Pilot**, and **Acura MDX**, the J35 has earned a reputation for longevity and low maintenance. Later versions, like the **J35Y**, introduced direct injection, DOHC architecture**, and improved emissions control, keeping the engine competitive in the modern era. It’s a workhorse with a refined edge—Honda engineering at its dependable best.
