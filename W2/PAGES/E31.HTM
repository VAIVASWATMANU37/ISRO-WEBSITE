Fiat FIRE 1.4 T-Jet

The Fiat FIRE 1.4 T-Jet is a turbocharged evolution of Fiat’s long-standing FIRE (Fully Integrated Robotised Engine) series, blending old-school robustness with modern forced induction. This 1.4-liter inline-four engine, known internally as the 198A4000, was produced from 2007 to 2020 and found in models like the Fiat Linea, Bravo, Grande Punto, and even Alfa Romeo MiTo and Giulietta. With a cast iron block, aluminum DOHC 16-valve head, and an IHI RHF3 VL37 turbocharger, it delivers around 120–155 horsepower and 206 Nm of torque, depending on the variant. The engine uses multi-point fuel injection, has hydraulic lifters, and lacks a phase regulator, which simplifies maintenance but limits variable valve timing. Despite its modest displacement, the T-Jet is known for its strong mid-range torque, making it lively in urban and highway driving. It’s also a favorite among tuners—capable of reaching 180–190 hp with bolt-on upgrades like intercoolers, remaps, and larger turbos. However, it’s not without quirks: turbo bypass valve diaphragm failures, oil separator membrane wear, and fuel pressure regulator issues can crop up, especially at higher mileage.
