Chevy LS series

The Chevy LS series is a family of V8 engines developed by General Motors that has become a modern icon in the performance and tuning world. Debuting in 1997 with the LS1 in the Corvette C5, the LS engines are known for their compact size, lightweight aluminum construction, and pushrod simplicity paired with modern fuel injection and ignition systems. Ranging from 4.8L to 7.0L, the LS family spans Gen III and Gen IV small-blocks, with power outputs from 255 to over 700 horsepower in factory form. Whether it's the high-revving LS6, the torque-rich LS3, or the supercharged LS9, the LS series has redefined what a modern V8 can be.
