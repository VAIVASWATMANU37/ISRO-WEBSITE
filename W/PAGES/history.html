HISTORY OF ISRO

HISTORY

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was founded in 1969 with the vision of harnessing space technology for national development. Spearheaded by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, ISRO evolved from the humble beginnings of launching small rockets in Thumba, Kerala, to becoming a global space power. The organization's breakthrough came with the Aryabhata satellite (1975), India's first indigenously developed satellite. Over the decades, ISRO achieved monumental milestones, including the Chandrayaan moon missions, the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), and advancements in satellite navigation, Earth observation, and interplanetary exploration. With innovations in launch vehicle technology and ambitious projects like Gaganyaan, India's first human spaceflight mission, ISRO continues to push the boundaries of space science, reinforcing India's position as a leader in global space exploration.
