HUMAN-SPACEFLIGHT PLANS OF ISRO

Human-Spaceflight

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is making significant strides in human spaceflight through its ambitious Gaganyaan mission. Scheduled for early 2027, Gaganyaan aims to send Indian astronauts into low Earth orbit (LEO) for a three-day mission, marking India's entry into independent human space exploration. The mission will utilize the Human-Rated LVM3 rocket, ensuring crew safety and reliability. ISRO has also collaborated with international space agencies, including NASA and Axiom Space, to train astronauts and refine mission protocols. The upcoming Axiom-4 mission, launching on June 10, 2025, will see Group Captain Shubhanshu Shukla travel to the International Space Station (ISS), gaining valuable experience for India's future crewed missions. With rigorous astronaut training, advanced life-support systems, and global partnerships, ISRO is paving the way for India's long-term human spaceflight ambitions, including space station development and interplanetary missions.
