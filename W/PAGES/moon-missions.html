MOON-MISSIONS OF ISRO

Moon-Missions

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has played a crucial role in lunar exploration with its ambitious Chandrayaan missions. Chandrayaan-1, launched in 2008, made history by discovering evidence of water molecules on the Moon, revolutionizing planetary science. Chandrayaan-2, launched in 2019, aimed for a soft landing on the Moon's south pole, although its lander faced last-minute challenges. Despite this, its orbiter continues to send valuable data. Chandrayaan-3, launched in 2023, successfully landed near the lunar south pole, making India the first country to achieve this feat. These missions demonstrate ISRO's growing expertise in interplanetary exploration and pave the way for future Moon missions, including collaborations and advanced robotic exploration.
