The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's premier space agency, renowned for its groundbreaking achievements in satellite technology, space exploration, and scientific research. Established in 1969, ISRO has transformed India into a global space power, launching communication, navigation, and Earth observation satellites that benefit diverse sectors like agriculture, disaster management, and environmental monitoring. Its successful missions, such as Chandrayaan, Mangalyaan, and Aditya-L1, have advanced planetary science and deep-space exploration. ISRO continues to push boundaries with future projects like Gaganyaan, India's first human spaceflight mission, and ambitious plans for lunar and interplanetary exploration, reinforcing its commitment to innovation and national development.
