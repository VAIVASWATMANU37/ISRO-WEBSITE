EARTH-OBSERVATION SATELLITES OF ISRO

Earth-Observation Satellites

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has developed a robust fleet of Earth observation satellites, providing critical data for agriculture, disaster management, environmental monitoring, and urban planning. Since the launch of IRS-1A in 1988, ISRO has built one of the largest constellations of remote sensing satellites, including Cartosat, RISAT, and EOS series, which deliver high-resolution imagery for mapping and surveillance. The HySIS satellite, launched in 2018, enhances hyperspectral imaging capabilities, supporting studies in forestry, coastal zones, and geological assessments. Recent missions like EOS-08, launched in 2024, incorporate advanced imaging technologies for disaster monitoring and environmental analysis. These satellites play a crucial role in India's scientific and strategic advancements, ensuring sustainable development and resource management.
