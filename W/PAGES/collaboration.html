The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has established extensive international collaborations, partnering with 61 countries and five multilateral organizations to advance space research and technology. ISRO works closely with leading agencies like NASA, CNES (France), JAXA (Japan), Roscosmos (Russia), and ESA (Europe) on joint missions, satellite development, and planetary exploration. Notable projects include NISAR, a radar imaging satellite developed with NASA, and TRISHNA, a thermal infrared imaging satellite in collaboration with CNES. ISRO also engages in capacity-building initiatives, supporting emerging space programs worldwide. Through these partnerships, ISRO strengthens India's global presence in space science, fostering innovation and technological exchange.
